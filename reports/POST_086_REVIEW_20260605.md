## What Actually Worked

The breakthrough was not local training. It was switching from local upload of huge zips to Kaggle remote-output submission:

```powershell
kaggle competitions submit nvidia-nemotron-model-reasoning-challenge -k <kernel_id> -f submission.zip -v <version> -m <message>
```

That let Kaggle submit an existing notebook output directly, avoiding local download and re-upload of 1GB to 3.5GB `submission.zip` files.

## What Did Not Work

Huikang v27 failed even when the notebook successfully generated a structurally valid flat zip. The likely issue is official evaluator compatibility, not basic zip structure. Repeating that route consumed time and produced no public score.

Kien output repack and Akihiko small adapter were structurally valid but scored `0.63` and `0.50`. They are not competitive and should not be repeated without a concrete mechanism change.

## Model And Tuning Boundary

The submitted artifacts are adapter packages. For routes #7 to #11, the scored packages came from public Kaggle Notebook outputs. We did not retrain those models locally.
